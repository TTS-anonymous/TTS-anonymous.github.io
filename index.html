Accelerating Autoregressive Speech Synthesis Inference With Speech Speculative Decoding

Interspeech 2025
Anonymous Authors

Abstract

Autoregressive speech synthesis models based on language models have achieved remarkable performance, but their inference speed remains constrained by the sequential nature of token prediction. In this work, we propose Speech Speculative Decoding (SSD), a novel framework that enhances speculative decoding to accelerate inference in autoregressive speech synthesis. Specifically, SSD employs a lightweight draft model to generate candidate token sequences in parallel, which are subsequently verified by the target model using an improved acceptance criterion specifically designed for the unique characteristics of speech tokens. Experimental results demonstrate that SSD achieves a significant speedup of 1.4x compared with conventional autoregressive decoding, while maintaining high fidelity and naturalness. Subjective evaluations further indicate that the speech generated by SSD aligns more closely with human auditory perception, validating the effectiveness of SSD.
